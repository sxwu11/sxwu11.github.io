EVALUATION METHODS

• DRINK IDENTIFIER - "I WANT EFFICIENT AND ACCURATE SERVICE"

Based on our findings from our user research, one of the primary customer pain points distills down to communication between them and the baristas. Between communicating orders and the transferring their name to be called by, customers have a hard time determining what the barista actually knows about their order. This also can be amplified by orders that involve passing along from the cashier to the barista that makes and delivers the order. The name miscommunication problem is so well known, that there is a certain irony with the restaurant with customer connection through individual names as a brand identity, is known for failing at this.

• PEAK HOUR PURCHASE - "I WANT TO MAKE GOOD USE OF MY TIME"

Our research indicated that sometimes consumers will need to wait for 20-30 minutes to get their drinks during peak hour, even if they place their orders in a short period of time. There is no way for them to know how long it takes barista to make their drinks. Due to the unpredictability of time needed, customers are hesitating if they should grab a coffee when seeing a long line during peak time.

• COFFEE JARGONS - "I DON'T ORDER FROM THE DEFAULT MENU ALL THE TIME"

Starbucks provides many coffee products, and has developed its own set of jargon. For people who are unfamiliar with coffee products but still go to Starbucks occasionally, they have difficulties in understanding these jargons. For frequent users, many of them only know about the things they order frequently, so they also need to struggle with the complicated Starbucks menu system and customization options.  All these factors will greatly slow down the purchase progress, which is especially bad during peak hours.

SOLUTION

We designed this kiosk prototype around a dynamic form, while also consolidating and simplifying features that the user will interact with into a cohesive form. Based on our user needs and preferences we determined a list of what would be needed in this kiosk. We determined that barcode and credit card scanning, as well as avatar and receipt printing would be required in addition to the touch screen interface.

• DIGITAL PROTOTYPE

We believe there is a wide playground for technological methods and lots of possibilities for in-store experience improvement with the application of a kiosk. Our current solution is an in-store Starbucks kiosk that enables customers to get accurate orders more effectively and pleasantly in store during peak hours.

At the first phase of our research, we used observations, semi-structured interviews, survey questions and literature reviews to better understand different purchasing contexts and identify potential user pain points at Starbucks.


Based on our observations and interviews, we choose to focus on physical store purchase at the register (either standard stores or reserves, whose purchasing experience are almost the same).  We believe there is a wider playground for technological methods and lots of possibilities for in-store experience improvement.

A checkout kiosk is a familiar idea for most users. Overall user satisfaction with the product. Almost all our test users responded positively on the designed interactions and they showed specific interests in the avatar generator at the checkout screen. Many believed the avatar generator is fun to use and will help with people with disabilities ordering coffee.



Based on our heuristic evaluations and benchmark testings, we found that our language used in the design, including both the icons and wording, should be expressed in clearer ways that users can better understand. We also need to give users more freedom of interacting with the interface. For example, we should make the avatar name optional for the users who would like to use their real names instead. We should also give users more freedom of editing their choices. For consistency and standards, we need to further test whether the categories we created appear to be consistent to users. For error prevention, we thought it might be better if we could show users the items that were just scanned.
