Populated area

The Haiti Summer Studio 2014 was challenged with designing solutions for the particular community of Petite-Rivière-de-Nippes.


Students visited the town, and communicated one-on-one with the residents, entrepreneurs, government officials, and NGOs, to develop an understanding of the daily workings of the community as well as to document its existing conditions and infrastructure.


The studio aimed to develop designs that could be constructed, efficiently used, and maintained by local individuals in Petite-Rivière-de-Nippes.

A MARKET HALL

Team design project

Neris Sandoval | Shengxi Wu


This proposal locates the market hall centrally within the core of Petite-Riviere-de-Nippes along the Departmentale Route 21. The advantage of this location is its proximity to the road leading south towards farmland and to the current heart of the town's street market.

MY ROLE

I spent one month at Haiti with my team conducting ethnographic research. We visited various places destroyed by the Haiti earthquake, interviewed local people and tried to understand the social context of Haiti.


Without the help of any tech tools, I documented the town with my team using the most basic tools. I created the town plan, researched local technologies and identified the design challenge with my team.


After returning campus, I designed the market with my teammate Neris with sustainable technologies. We presented our design to NGOs and used it as way for funding opportunities.

User Experience Researcher | Architectural/Visual Designer

THE TOWN

To understand the physical condition of Petite-Rivière-de-Nippes, the group broke ourselves into smaller teams and documented the town on foot. By hand drawing maps to locate and categorize buildings, we gained an understanding of the physical tapestry of the town, such as the limits of existing infrastructure and planning efforts, the nature of education and entrepreneurial activity, local customs and culture, and the needs and desires of residents and government officials in the community.
