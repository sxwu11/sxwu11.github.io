ASSUMPTIONS & INTEGRATION

With all the design implications above in mind, we decided to build an Alexa skill that provides suggested recipes to users based on the food they have in a smart fridge, and orders missing ingredients from Amazon fresh.


One such assumption we made is that future smart fridges would be able to recognize what's in them, and whether the food/ingredients are about to expire. This technology can be realized by adding a tag to each product we buy to encode information about product name & expiration date, or by using computer vision algorithms to detect products within the fridge and estimate whether they are about to expire. Several products from Samsung and Whirlpool with similiar ideas were later presented at CES 2018, demonstrating the feasibility of our concept.

ANALYSIS

VOICE INTERACTION

By using a natural language interface, we aim to reduce the cognitive load for users, who are already preoccupied with different tasks in their lives. Users will not have to look into the fridge, think about what they can do with the existing ingredients, and then find recipes online. Neither will they need to touch their smartphones or computers to follow the recipes when their hands are wet, or after they handle raw meat, poultry, fish, and egg products.


For this design exercise, we believe cooking will be a good scenario to implement voice interaction because of the following reason.

Voice User Interface Allows an Eyes & Hands-Free Interaction Experience

Considering users’ eyes and hands are usually occupied with cooking tasks. Voice user interface becomes a great interaction solution.

According to research, dictating is, on average, three times faster than typing. Rather than navigating through a graphical user interface. Voice interaction almost requires no digital literacy from user which means anyone can understand it quickly and interact with it naturally.

Voice User Interface Saves Time and Increases Work Productivity

Voice User Interface Allows Greater Mobility

Interact with voice allows user to dictate on the go and give them greater mobility. This is particularly useful in kitchen context considering user might constantly move between equipments.

According to Amazon's VUI Design guidelines:

Alexa is a persona, not the device itself.

• Speaking naturally and conversationally

• Having variation in the responses given

• Engaging user with questions

• Staying true to Alexa's personality

Guiding principles of Alexa skill experiences:

• Alexa's personality is friendly, upbeat, and helpful

• She can handle daily tasks with ease and accuracy

• She's honest about anything blocking her way, but fun and personable

• She is able to make small talk without being obtrusive or inappropriate

STORYBOARD

SCENE 1/8

The system will accurately identify what products are available in the fridge and proactively search for recipes that contain those products. It will provide users with several options and rank them using different criteria, including user's preference, available ingredients in fridge, etc.

SCENE 2/8

Users will be able to quickly order the missing ingredients with Alexa through Amazonfresh. The system will place the order and have them delivered within hours (dependent on the efficiency of the external delivery service).

SCENE 3/8

Once the ingredients has been delivered, users can continue interacting with Alexa by providing deliver confirmation. The system will then asked users if they need cooking guidance.

SCENE 4/8

Users will have the option to follow any recipe step by step provided by Alexa. They will not have to memorize every step at once. Instead, they get instructions on what to do at the moment via voice output.

SCENE 5/8

Users will complete the whole process without having to take out their smartphone, tablet or computer and interact with a visual interface by hand.

IDEAL CONVERSATION

We started by imagining a series of use case senarios/storyboards of a user interacting with Cook Fresh. Then we designed an ideal conversation interaction between them. With Alexa Design Guide in mind, we tried to make sure each of Cook Fresh's response sounds natural & concise to our users so that it can provide enough information to successfully engage the user in a conversation, and that it can guide the user's response without explicitly telling him/her what to say.

IDEATE

SYNONYMS (WORK IN PROGRESS)

Humans are not machine. They might express same ideas with various wording. In order to make the conversation more human and natural, we will need to brainstorm possible synonyms that people would use for each input.

CONVERSATION DETOUR (WORK IN PROGRESS)

Coversations are usually informal: it could branch out into various detailed conversation even in a same context. As a result, when user reply with what we did not expect in the ideal flow, we must design corresponding responses and take care as much edge cases as possible to maintain the conversation so that the interaction feels natural.
