ASSUMPTIONS & INTEGRATION

With all the design implications above in mind, we decided to build an Alexa skill that provides suggested recipes to users based on the food they have in a smart fridge, and orders missing ingredients from Amazon fresh.


One such assumption we made is that future smart fridges would be able to recognize what's in them, and whether the food/ingredients are about to expire. This technology can be realized by adding a tag to each product we buy to encode information about product name & expiration date, or by using computer vision algorithms to detect products within the fridge and estimate whether they are about to expire. Several products from Samsung and Whirlpool with similiar ideas were later presented at CES 2018, demonstrating the feasibility of our concept.

ANALYSIS

VOICE INTERACTION

Research have shown that using a natural language interface could reduce the cognitive load for users. It could be particularly helpful for senarios where users are already preoccupied with different tasks. This has been proved by a survey did by Statista.


For this design exercise, we believe cooking will be a good scenario to implement voice interaction due to the following reasons.

Voice User Interface allows eyes & hands-free interaction

Considering users’ eyes and hands are usually occupied with cooking tasks. Voice user interface could be helpful because they no loger need to touch their smartphones or computers to follow the recipes when their hands are wet, or after they handle raw meat, poultry, fish, and egg products.

According to research, dictating is, on average, three times faster than typing. Rather than navigating through a graphical user interface. Voice interaction almost requires no digital literacy from user which means anyone can understand it quickly and interact with it naturally.

Voice User Interface saves time and increases work productivity

Voice User Interface allows greater mobility

Interact with voice allows user to dictate on the go and give them greater mobility. This is particularly useful in kitchen context considering user might constantly move between equipments.

According to Amazon's VUI Design guidelines:

Alexa is a persona, not the device itself.

• Speaking naturally and conversationally

• Having variation in the responses given

• Engaging user with questions

• Staying true to Alexa's personality

Guiding principles of Alexa skill experiences:

• Alexa's personality is friendly, upbeat, and helpful

• She can handle daily tasks with ease and accuracy

• She's honest about anything blocking her way, but fun and personable

• She is able to make small talk without being obtrusive or inappropriate

STORYBOARD

SCENE 1/8

The system will accurately identify what products are available in the fridge and proactively search for recipes that contain those products. It will provide users with several options and rank them using different criteria, including user's preference, available ingredients in fridge, etc.

SCENE 2/8

Users will be able to quickly order the missing ingredients with Alexa through Amazonfresh. The system will place the order and have them delivered within hours (dependent on the efficiency of the external delivery service).

SCENE 3/8

Once the ingredients has been delivered, users can continue interacting with Alexa by providing deliver confirmation. The system will then asked users if they need cooking guidance.

SCENE 4/8

Users will have the option to follow any recipe step by step provided by Alexa. They will not have to memorize every step at once. Instead, they get instructions on what to do at the moment via voice output.

Users will complete the whole process without having to take out their smartphone, tablet or computer and interact with a visual interface by hand.

IDEAL CONVERSATION

We started by imagining a series of use case senarios/storyboards of a user interacting with Cook Fresh. Then we designed an standard conversation interaction between them. With Alexa Design Guide in mind, we tried to make sure each of Cook Fresh's response sounds natural & concise to our users so that it can provide enough information to successfully engage the user in a conversation, and that it can guide the user's response without explicitly telling him/her what to say.

EVALUATION

On-boarding experience

With limited time to spend on this project, we only brought our prototype to few users to help testing the conversation during leisure time without having them testing it in a real contextual environment. We will need to collect data by conducting more user testing sessions in order to further refine our design and conversation flow.

We made assumptions that users have already gone through the initial setup processes and linked Amazon Fresh and smart fridge with Cook Fresh. In fact, it is likely that they will need to go through necessary steps to set up services, grant permissions and establish user profiles before they could use the skill. This includes but not limited to: billing information, shipping address, shopping preferences, dietary preferences, which could be cumbersome. We would work on the initial on-boarding experience if time permits.

AFTERTHOUGHTS

We interacted with voice assistant like Siri and Google Assistant before we started this exercise. Although they could response to most of the basic questions pretty well, sometimes it is still quite difficult for them to respond appropriately according to the context in many cases. Since current technology could not support free conversation, as a designer, we need to design the questions so that it elicit certain types of responses from the users without explicitly telling the users what to do.


CONSTRAINTS OF VOICE USER INTERFACE

Respond contextually

Tolerance

VUI allows user to interact with machine in a more natural way and could be really handy under certain circumstance. However, it is usually less efficient in carrying & conveying the same amount of information compared to GUI: people could easily miss or forget keywords and thus need to repeat a process. As a result, VUI designer should design the interaction in such a way that it only provides the most necessary information at the moment. Meanwhile, breaking comprehensive question into step-by-step simple and short questions would also help reduce cognitive overload.


VARIATIONS

Humans are not machine. They might express same ideas with various wording. In order to make the conversation more human and natural, we will need to design from user's perspective and try to brainstorm possible synonyms that they would use for each input.

DETOUR

Coversations are usually informal: it could branch out into unexpected conversation even in a same context. As a result, when user reply with what we did not expect in the ideal flow, we must design corresponding responses and take care as much edge cases as possible to maintain the conversation so that the interaction feels natural.


Due to the given time limit and non-exhaustive nature of possible responses, we were not able to incorporate every case into our design for this design exercise. However, this exercise helped us learnt how to design from a user's perspective / design voice feedback with possible input from users.

REFINE VOICE INTERACTIONS

Based on the standard conversation and use case senarios, we further developed and added two main functions:


    Ordering missing ingredients




    Food expiration reminder


Alexa is able to order missing ingredients from Amazon fresh and continue conversation once the ingredient arrived. Users will be required to link the skill to their Amazon Fresh account, and give the skill access to their mailing address.

Alexa is able to identify food ingredients that are going to expire and suggest recipes to reduce food waste. We made the assumption that future smart fridges would be able to recognize what's in them, and whether the food/ingredients are about to expire.
