Poster design for the 2017 Georgia Tech smart cities speaker series that communicates the program of events and visually communicates a message about the topic of smart cities.These poster design demonstrated an understanding of basic elements and principles of visual design through typography and illustration.

The North Avenue Smart Corridor of Atlanta will be the most connected corridor in the state of Georgia, serving as a living lab for active, multi-modal traffic management through technology deployment and data analytics. Smart technology solutions along the North Avenue corridor will improve connectivity, roadway and public safety, mobility, and the environment.


For local governments, a pressing challenge related to the corridor is converting these short-term investments in ongoing and long-term revenue for a city or region. Revenue can come from real estate development and investment to population growth (e.g. increased tax base) to company relocation (consider the fervor related to the location of Amazon’s new headquarters). As such, the corridor contributes to inter-urban competition, and so are branded to attract investors, resettle workers, and establish burge oning business districts.


Below are the identity system I created for Atlanta’s North Avenue Smart Corridor. This design tries to emphasize the CONNECTIVITY of the smart corridor. The identity system includes a logo and alternative mark, color palette, informational icons (for identifying amenities/projects), ,street signs and banner, website homepage and app icon.
