I. JOB SITE SECURITY

To execute an on-site observation, architects need to bring architectural drawings, camera and sketch book to the site. They need to climb ladders, reach to rooftop, walk across beams and avoid other obstacles to perform the check. This adds uncertainties to the safety of  the architect, who will need to carry all these necessities while walking on the job site.

II. PAYROLL

Traditional way of on-site documentation usually requires at least two well-experienced architect to carry out the tasks: usually one to identify problems and the other annotates the drawings. Such method is inefficient and will add extra labor cost to the design firm.

$

III. MISUNDERSTANDING

Architects tend to use their own signs and languages to annotate the drawings with the problem identified. However, contractor might have problem understanding the handwriting or verbiage of the architect. This will extend the punchlist correction process and increase the risk of construction delay.
