LITERATURE REVIEW

The goal of our team is to try to understand how overseas students eat every day on campus. Our group conducted literature review and user interview. We decided to focus particularly on overseas Chinese student due to the following facts of this user group.

RESEARCH

Academic stress

Chinese students cite academic pressure as the most likely cause of stress. They are often found carrying the burden of immense expectations of their families. Therefore, overseas Chinese student tend to spend more time dealing with study and thus spend less energy on other daily tasks like cooking.

Chinese students acknowledge cultural barrier as one big challenge of living abroad. The experience of moving into a country with a history, culture, and language so different from their own can be overwhelming. Based on our user interviews, dining is a typical example. Many interviewees expressed that they have difficulties adapting to the new dining culture.

Cultural barrier

Independent living skills

Chinese parents highly expect for their children's education. The role parents play in family is to provide the food and take the empty bowls away without disturbing all the homework. As such, some of them might lack independent living skills when they live away from parents. This is particularly a fact for Chinese students studying abroad.

I am not good at cooking so I rely on restaurant a lot. Sometimes I order more than one take-out meals from a restaurant to save time and delivery cost.

I only prepare food on my own when I have time, but it takes efforts: you cook for two hours then finish the meal in ten minutes. I really do not have time for that during finals.

Due to the nature of this user group, time and cost are the two keywords they mentioned most. There is a tendency that they will spend time cooking to save money when they are free, while spend money to buy time when they are getting busy. SURVEY

In order to get better understanding of the dining preference of overseas Chinese students, we conducted a survey with list of questions to learn the dining habits of this user group and come up with critical findings below.

Overseas Chinese students are seeking more dining choices

According to survey result, 73% of the survey participants had experience in using food delivery services as an alternative. They believe such service provides a wider range of selection with comparatively higher cost.


Although on-campus dining could offer quick and dirty dining solution, lacks of choices and unsatisfied taste were two main complaints. While off-campus dining could provide better experience, they usually cost much and were not very accessible.

EVALUATION

Our team conducted 3 heuristic evaluation sessions and 20 usability testing sessions for each iterations. The improving System usability scale(SUS) scores demonstrated that our design iterations are more and more intuitive, efficient, and able to meet users’ needs.

AFTERTHOUGHTS

This is my first time working with developers. I am really impressed by how a developer approaches a problem differently from a designer. They tend to focus on functionality and efficiency while solving problems and do not understand the importance of iterative design. Many of them believe that user experience design is equal to user interface design which could be done in the very last minute. Therefore, it is really important to get developers involved in early design phase, educate them the importance of design iteration.


As designers, we fail in understanding the design limitations of WeChat mini-program and waste a lot of time due to lack of communication with developers at the beginning. I begin to understand that design is about communicating in the most effective and appropriate way possible. Establishing effective communication between developer and designer is critical for any product design team.

Working with developers

Consider all stakeholders

Designing a service system requires input from all stakeholders. As UX designer, we should bring skills and approaches that not only balance complex stakeholders' requirements but also create embodied solutions to meet these needs. To achieve the best solution, acknowledgment and input from multiple stakeholders are essential to our decision-making.

Order Matching

• CUSTOMER

• RESTAURANT OWNER

Restaurant owner indicated sometimes customer did not show up at the pick-up location. The driver waited for a long time and had to take the food back. This lower their delivery efficiency and further resulted in complaints from next delivery customer.

Most of our users said they've had bad experience while waiting for the arrival of their orders. They had difficulities finding the delivery driver and describing the location, especially when they are not quite familiar with surroundings.

We discovered that there existed an informal food delivery service on WeChat at Georgia Tech. Restaurant set up direct contact with customers in WeChat group, collected orders through instant messaging and distributed food at a fixed spot. However, as an instant messenger, WeChat is not professional as a food delivery platform, which resulted in bad user experience.

WeChat - a compromised solution

• CUSTOMER

• RESTAURANT OWNER

Restaurant loved to use WeChat since there is no set up or membership fee required. Similarly however, as a compromised solution. It was really hard for restaurant owner to keep track of delivery status and revenues using this unprofessional alternative.

User complained that they might easily missed pick-up notification since all notifications are distributed in a chat group. There was no way for them to manage or track their order through an instant messenger app.

Affordability

• CUSTOMER

• RESTAURANT OWNER

Restaurant owner complained that the membership fee and service cost of joining major food delivery platform are too high. If they would like to provide food delivery service and make profit, they have to pass these extra costs on to the customers.

Affordability are most frequent complaints we heard regarded to popular food delivery services. Most of our users said they paid extra attention on delivery cost. Some even compared prices on different platforms to find the best deal.

Improve the affordability and accessibility of food delivery services

while maintaining the profitability of restaurants.

BRAINSTORMING

Since most of our users complain about order matching and affordability, we conduct a brainstorming session and come up with a lot of features and ideas to tackle these two major problems.

WECHAT MINI-PROGRAM

We believe there is a design opportunity to build a WeChat Mini Program that incorporates relevant food delivery features to improve user experience. The advantages are listed below.

No migration cost / setup required

Similiar as using an application on Facebook, while using WeChat Mini program, existing WeChat user will not need to download separate application or create a new account, which creates better on-boarding experience.

Concentration of critical mass

Nowadays, almost all Chinese people have their own WeChat account. Since we are focusing on overseas Chinese student as our user. Building on WeChat will help us guarantee the access from critical mass.

We realized that many e-commerce platform nowadays such as Taobao, Pinduoduo, and Meituan, allowing users to participate in collective buying. Research has shown that this business model would help indivudial saves money on purchase while increasing the overall profit of business owners. Therefore, we decided to brainstorm how to implement foodpool and narrow down to ideas below.
