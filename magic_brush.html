3 MINUTES INTRODUCTION

Maliang and the Magic Brush is a VR experiment designed for the HTC Vive and other room-scale VR devices. It was inspired by Google Tilt brush,

a VR game that allows interactors to paint in an immersive 3D virtual space. Our team aims to take such experiences further allowing interactors not just to paint, but to instantiate and interact with the objects they create in virtual space. We believe such instantiated experience is a good fit for VR narrative, affording interactors the experience of dramatic agency.

INTERACTION INSPIRATION

“Magic Brush” is a Chinese traditional fairy tale. It is about a kind little boy Maliang who has a magic brush that can turn anything he draws into a real world artifact.


Our narrative will take cues from this ancient Chinese fairy tale. Interactors will be able to instantiate what they drawn in the 3D virtual space.

DRAW

Interaction inspired by Google Tilt Brush

Tile Brush allows user to paint in an immersive virtual 3D spaces. Users are presented with a virtual palette from which they can select from a variety of brush types and colors. Movement of the handheld controller in 3D space creates brush strokes that follow in the virtual environment.


In our design, we would like to further improve such drawing/creating interaction to give our interactors dramatic agency.

INSTANTIATE

Narrative & dramatic agency inspired by "Magic Brush"

Harold is a curious little boy who, with his purple crayon, has the power to create a world of his own simply by drawing it. He wants to go for a walk in the moonlight, but there is no moon, so he draws one. He has nowhere to walk, so he draws a path.


Inspired by the story, we want interactors not only being able to instantiate the objects they drawn, but also able to interact with the objects they drawn.

SOUL KEEPER

Soul Keeper is a virtual reality game where you can be a wizard and cast magic spells at enemy creatures. In the game, interactors use controllers to draw symbols and trigger corresponding spells. Each spell will be triggered by a different shapes and has a different way of interacting with hands. For example, fireballs will be triggered by drawing a square and can be thrown like baseballs and fly depending on how hard you throw them.

INTERACTION DESIGN

For our initial round of user testing, our team decided to use semi-transparent strokes floating in the virtual space as visual cues to guide interactors throughout the narrative. Interactors will need to follow the cue, make sure what they drawn using the controllers are overlapped with the cues. Then they will trigger the instantiation of an in-game objects.


We will observe our testers' behavior, collect user feedbacks and conduct qualitative study to see how to better design visual cues to guide our interactors' in-game behavior in our next iterations.
